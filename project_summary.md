# AI-Assisted Food Safety Risk Assessment Workflow

## Project Overview

This project explores the use of structured prompt engineering and AI-assisted scientific reasoning to support contamination risk assessment in food production systems.

The workflow integrates concepts from:
- food microbiology
- HACCP systems
- environmental monitoring
- contamination pathway analysis
- Food Safety Management Systems (FSMS)

The project was designed as a practical scientific workflow prototype aligned with applied food technology and food safety analysis.

---

# Objectives

- Develop structured contamination risk assessment workflows
- Apply HACCP-oriented reasoning to food production systems
- Analyze contamination pathways and environmental risks
- Organize scientific outputs using standardized analytical frameworks
- Demonstrate applied microbiology systems thinking

---

# Scientific Scope

The workflow focuses on:
- biological contamination hazards
- contamination pathway mapping
- HACCP Critical Control Point (CCP) analysis
- Environmental Monitoring Programs (EMP)
- microbiological detection strategies
- risk characterization
- root cause analysis
- risk escalation logic

---

```text
Food Production Scenario
```

# Case Studies

The workflow was validated across multiple food production systems:

### 1. Ready-to-Eat Cold-Smoked Salmon

Focus:
- Listeria monocytogenes
- post-processing contamination
- cold-chain management
- environmental persistence

### 2. Ready-to-Eat Leafy Greens

Focus:
- Escherichia coli O157:H7
- irrigation contamination
- wash-water cross contamination
- agricultural risk pathways

### 3. Powdered Infant Formula

Focus:
- Cronobacter sakazakii
- dry-environment contamination
- powder handling systems
- environmental ingress control

# Scientific Limitations

This workflow is intended for educational and exploratory food safety analysis only.

The project does not include:
- laboratory validation
- predictive microbiology modeling
- industrial production datasets
- regulatory certification capability

Outputs require scientific verification and are not intended for direct regulatory decision-making.
